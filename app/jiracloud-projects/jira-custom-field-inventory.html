Problem

Jira instances often accumulate hundreds (or thousands) of custom fields over time. Without an inventory, teams struggle to understand which fields are actually used, where they appear, and who owns them. This creates governance risk, breaks reporting consistency, and becomes a blocker during Jira Cloud/DC migrations.

Main idea

Build an automated inventory job that extracts custom field metadata from Jira, enriches it with usage and configuration context, and exports a structured CSV. The output becomes the single source of truth for governance, cleanup, and migration planning.

What the automation handles
  • Discovery: scans all custom fields in the Jira instance.
  • Metadata: captures name, ID, type, and configuration details.
  • Context: maps fields to relevant screens / contexts (where applicable).
  • Governance support: highlights candidates for cleanup or standardization.
  • Export: writes a structured CSV for audit and migration workstreams.

Outcomes
  • Created a single source of truth for custom fields across the Jira instance.
  • Enabled governance cleanup decisions (remove, merge, standardize) with evidence.
  • Reduced migration risk by identifying fields and configurations ahead of time.
  • Improved reporting consistency by detecting redundant or misconfigured fields.
